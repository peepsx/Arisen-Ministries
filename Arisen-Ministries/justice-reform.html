Fighting for justice reform.

Learn more about our fight for criminal justice reform and prison reform in one of the world's most destructive justice systems.

Learn more

Criminal justice reforms.

In most cases, especially at the federal level, individuals who do not pose a threat to the general public are held without bond for non-violent charges and locked away with violent offenders. This forces many non-violent offenders to plead guilty to charges they're not guilty of, in hopes they will be transferred to a minimum security prison to escape the violence of the maximum security detainment center in which they are being held. This is only one of many issues within our criminal justice system and why we are actively fighting for ethical reforms to America's criminal justice system.

keyboard_arrow_down

Placing technology in the prison.

In America's prisons, not only are prisoners subjected to an environment in which there is nothing to do, education programs are outdated and practically useless in a world that's advancing at rates never before seen in human history. Bringing tech programs into prisons would create a safer environment for guards and prisoners alike, while allowing prisoners to build a future for themselves in areas that actually matter. It may be surprising how many of our future computer engineers could be found within our prison system in America.

keyboard_arrow_down

Expanding prison education programs.

Expanding tech-based education programs in prison is simply not enough, considering there are so many career paths in the real world. Pushing our federal government to partner with universities so that prisoners have the ability to earn a degree from behind bars, would make the country a safer place and bring recidivism rates to record lows.

keyboard_arrow_down

Ending prison violence.

By providing prisoners with the ability to mold a future with real and authentic pathways, while reforming guidelines that allow for the release of prisoners who successfully earn a degree, prisoners will be less likely to join gangs and prison violence would decrease significantly as a result. Sadly, many prison employees promote violence and ultimately enable it - rather than trying to help reform the prison population. It's why so many people suffer life-altering injuries behind bars and why so many prisoners join gangs for protection. We're fighting to end that reality.
